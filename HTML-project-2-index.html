Nissan Skyline GTR R32 (1989-1994)

History & Base Stats

The R32 is the third generation of Nissan's "Skyline GT-R" branch of cars. This car, as well as all others on this list is a very sought after car for japanese car enthusiasts around the world. The R32 is a 2-door, front engine, all wheel drive car. Stock, this car comes with a 2.6 litre twinturbo engine with a 5-speed manual transmission. Over the course of 28 years since this car has stopped production the price has skyrocketed and they are becoming harder to get every month. On release, the R32 was origionally $31,000-$35,000 USD. Now, if you get lucky enough to find one on the market, it will be anywhere from $40,000-$50,000 for a stock model.

Nissan Skyline GTR R33 (1995-1998)

History & Base Stats

The R33 is the fourth generation of Nissan's "Skyline GT-R" branch or cars and in my opinion one of the coolest. This car, on the same terms as the R32 is also very popular now and when it was released. The R33 is a 2-door or 4-door, front engine, all wheel drive car. The base version of this car comes with a 2.6 litre twinturbo engine with a 5-speed manual transmission. Similar to the R32 this cars price has also jumped since it has stopped production in 1998. The price of this car on release was $33,000 USD and now you can get one for around $40,000-$60,000 USD depending on the type.

Nissan Skyline GTR R34 (1999-2002)

History & Base Stats

The R34 is the fifth and one of the most popular productions from Nissan's "Skyline GT-R" line of cars. Being one of the newer versions of the GT-R, the R34 is more relatable to new generations of car enthusiasts but still has that oldschool feel to it making it also very popular. This car has similar specs to the previous ones coming stock with a 2.6 litre twinturbo engine (the same engine as the last two cars) and a 6-speed manual transmission. This car was originally $45,000 USD from japan and now you can get one for around $120,000 USD for a used base model making it the most expensive car on this list so far.

Nissan Skyline GTR R35 (2009-Present)

History & Base Stats

The R35 is the sixth generation of Nissan's "Skyline GT-R" line of cars. Being the newest car on this list, the R45 is the best car for any new car enthusiast as it suits to the newer tastes of people of this generation. The only issue with this car is that it is completely redesigned and does not take after the nostalgic feeling of the older cars which many car people do not like about it. A stock R35 comes with an all new twinturbo engine that is much better than the old one. It also has a 6-speed automatic transmission with a front engine all wheel drive setup. The original versions of this car from 2007 cost you around $70,000 USD and the newer ones from 2021-2022 will cost you $110,000-130,000 USD making some classify it as a supercar.

Summary



Overall, the four most popular generations of the Nissan Skyline GT-R are all awesome cars. The R32-R34 are great for people who want that original feeling with the RB26DETT I6 engine. The R35 is a great car for the people who want something newer and more powerful as its engine is almost twice as powerful as the original engine from the last three cars. When picking your GT-R you should go based off of your opinions and what kind of a car you want to drive and own. Just know that no one at a car meet will judge what car you have if you are driving the famous Nissan Skyline GT-R.




  • The GT-R R32 is, old, cool, and very fun to drive.
  • The GT-R R33 is, old but newer, very cool, and fun to drive.
  • The GT-R R34 is a newer car, has newer features, but still looks older.
  • The GT-R R35 is new, cool, powerful, and fun to drive, but very expensive.
